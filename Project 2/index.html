Welcome to Gothic Art

Gothic art, a defining movement in medieval European culture, emerged in the 12th century and flourished until the 16th century. Known for its emphasis on verticality, light, and ornamentation, Gothic art continues to inspire admiration for its achievements in art and architecture.

Gothic Architecture

Gothic architecture is recognizable for pointed arches, ribbed vaults, and flying buttresses. These innovations allowed taller structures with stained glass windows. Iconic examples include the Notre-Dame Cathedral in Paris and Chartres Cathedral.

Gothic Paintings

Gothic paintings depicted religious themes, using vibrant colors, gold leaf, and intricate details to evoke emotional storytelling and dramatic intensity.

Gothic Sculpture

Gothic sculptures adorned churches, with figures of saints, biblical scenes, and gargoyles combining realism and symbolism.
